Example

Note that the converse is not true. For example, taking un=(−1)nu_{n} = (-1)^{n}un​=(−1)n, the Cesàro averages converge to 000, but (un)(u_{n})(un​) does not converge. Cesàro's lemma is very useful when applied to telescoping sequences. If uku_{k}uk​ converges to 000, it is sometimes easy to show that vk≐uk+1α−ukαv_{k} \doteq u_{k+1}^{\alpha} - u_{k}^{\alpha}vk​≐uk+1α​−ukα​ converges to a non-zero limit ℓ\ellℓ for some appropriate choice of α<0\alpha < 0α<0.
